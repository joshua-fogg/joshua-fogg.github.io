Problem Solving Techniques and How I've Used Them

  • Pseudocode

    I have found this great for clarifying what it is I am trying to achieve. I find it especially useful when I lose track of what certain sections of code are doing. It's a ncie way to document it like a walkthrough for easy guidance.

  • Trying something

    has got to be one of the easiest but often forgotten methods. Often I have found that experimenting is the answer because with each failed attempt I get another step closer to understanding how it works. It does require determination and a creative approach though.

  • Rubber ducky method

    aka dumbing it down and making it clear. Pseudocode is great for this and as the Wiki states often just by talking about it the user tends to come across the solution in the process of explaining the problem.

  • Reading error messages

    can be a good way to increase your understanding as they do give a good idea as to what is going on but often it requires a certain leevel of background knowledge to be able to figure it out. However googleing the error can give great results.

  • Console.logging

    helps generate some more 'experimental data' as it were. Console logging allows you to create a list of what issues arrive... I dont use this... like at all.

  • Googling

    definately has to be the most used method I use to seek inspiration and solutions or advice when I am stuck.

  • Asking your peers for help

    is also highly useful. The amount of knowledge and expertise in a group can prove suprising.

  • Asking coaches for help

    yes this is a good way to go. Although I do like to have a good tinker attmept myself first so that I can analyse what the coach does and try to find a learning from it to expand my toolbelt.

  • Improving your process with reflection

    is a great way to go as it often lets you explore new approaches by bringing additional data to the forefront. I often use this technique with others to achieve a solution.
