Mindfull Conversations: Natural: Informal practice; adopt a generous attitude by gifting the speaker your attention. Artificial: Formal Practice; construct conversations to train the 3 skills for mindful listening. At the end it allows discussion of the talk.

Mindfullness Techniques: 1. mindful listening: Allows frienships to be established in 6 minutes. 2. looping: Collaborative project where 2 or more people work together to help the listeners fully understand the speaker. 3. Dippig: Self-awareness during listening.

Any other musings?


  • Mindful Conversation is most useful when communication is at an impasse, i.e conflict.
  • three parts to this skill are listening, looping, and dipping.
  • Questions to utilise at a regualr interval with a mindfulness buddy:
    • How am I doing with my commitment to my practice?
    • What has arisen in my life that relates to my practice?
    • how did this conversation go?
  • Do less than you can: keep it enjoyable and easy
  • Take one breath a day: start with small easily achievable goals to build and reinforce a routine.
  • Somtimes not trying hard can give the greatest yeild
  • The better you are at letting go, the better you are at both meditating and falling asleep.
  • Have expectations before meditation, but have no expectation during meditation.
  • Sukha is a quality of joy not requiring energy. it is so subtle that it takes a very quiet mind to access
  • Meditation Circuit Training
  • meditation progress
    • Initial Access Phase: (Gradual Effort phase) able to access a certain state of mind but it is easily lost.
    • Lag Phase: not much changes despite effort
    • Consolidation Phase: (Sudden Enlightenment phase) boom pahse where on demand state of mind state. often follows a long lag phase
