    Mindfulness and Self-Awareness?

    Mindfulness is a process of the psyche where by you pay attention in the present moment, non-judgmentally. This is akin to the flagpole in Chade-Meng's analogy from Search Inside Yourself below.
    Self-awareness is a neutral mode that maintains self-reflectiveness even in the midst of turbulent emotions. This is akin to the flag in the below example.
    Both are trainable.

    Tan, Chade-Meng(2012):
    "The traditional analogy of this mind is a fluttering flag on a flagpole. The flag represents the mind. In the presence of strong emotions, the mind may be turbulent like a flag fluttering violently in the wind."

    Self awareness practices from chapter 4 (Body Scan, Journaling). What was your experience of it? How did you feel?

    Body scan – Can be done during meditation and requires no additional tools. It is where you systemically bring full attention to different parts of the body starting from the top of your body and gradually sweep down the other end of your body. It is ideal to finish with thinking about a positive emotion.


    Journaling – requires tools to record your thoughts on paper. It's likened to uncorking your brain and allowing your thoughts to flow onto the paper infront of you. This method often helps identify emotional themes and allows clarify ideas on paper. It is important to start with a prompt, which can start a theme of thought. Prompts can include:

    • What I am feeling now is…
    • I am aware that…
    • What motivates me is…
    When I have done this in the past I have often felt like a load has been taken off by the end of it.

    I can see how both of these methods and practices are beneficial and rewarding. I do admit I find these hard to sit down and commit to, I feel a bit silly doing so unless there is something big getting to me. I have found in the past these methods rewarding, typically if I am in a private space then I will do journalling as I can write things on paper and get rid of them at the end. I have found the Body-Scan useful with martial arts and sports as it helps me to feel out and be aware off any injuries I have developed during practice. I find this method most useful for reminding myself to go do something physical or stretching as I become aware of lethargy or stiffness in me from sitting on a computer.
